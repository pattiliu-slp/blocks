About

Patricia Liu

I'm Patricia, a speech-language pathologist specializing in acquired neurologic communication disorders in adults. I work with individuals and families at some of the most challenging moments of their lives — when a diagnosis changes how someone speaks, moves, or thinks.

My clinical work spans communication and cognitive rehabilitation, AAC and speech-generating device funding and implementation, and supporting patients and families as they navigate the evolving challenges of progressive neurologic conditions. I meet people across the continuum — from early diagnosis through significant changes in function — and adapt my approach as their needs shift over time.

I'm also deeply curious about the role emerging technologies can play in our field — from tools that help clinicians deliver more efficient, individualized care to innovations that open new pathways for individuals to connect and communicate more fully.

I believe in honest conversations, and in care that meets people where they are — and helps them reclaim their voice and agency.

Areas of Expertise

Speech, Language & Cognitive-Communication Disorders

These are the ways a neurologic condition can affect someone's ability to speak, find words, or express their thoughts.

  • Aphasia
    Difficulty with spoken or written language, often following stroke or brain injury
  • Dysarthria
    Changes in speech clarity or strength due to muscle weakness or reduced motor control
  • Apraxia of Speech
    Difficulty coordinating the movements needed for speech, separate from muscle weakness
  • Cognitive-Communication Disorders
    Challenges with attention, memory, organization, or reasoning that affect how someone communicates

Neurologic Conditions

Tools & Projects

Clinical Tools

Things I made out of curiosity because they didn't exist yet. Free to use — feedback always welcome.

Connected Speech Analysis

CIU Calculator

A tool for scoring Correct Information Units (CIUs) from connected speech samples, based on the Nicholas & Brookshire (1993) method. Paste in a transcript or record directly in the browser using voice-to-text, then run an analysis that outputs word count, CIU count, % CIUs, words per minute, and CIUs per minute. Manually tag words that don't qualify as CIUs based on your knowledge of the task and the patient — the tool handles the counting, you bring the clinical judgment.

Try it →

Word-Finding Treatment

Lexical Retrieval Treatment Practice

A customizable home practice tool for Lexical Retrieval Treatment, built with PPA patients in mind. Upload personal images from your device or paste image URLs, then work through semantic, phonemic, orthographic, and repetition self-cueing. Designed to solve the perennial problem of building personally-relevant practice materials without putting that burden on caregivers — or back on you.

Try it →

Aphasia Treatment

Circumlocution & Semantic Feature Practice

Choose a word set — high or low frequency, concrete or abstract — or enter your own. Target words are presented one at a time, and the user describes the word while an AI makes real-time guesses. Success means providing enough context for the AI to identify the target. A more dynamic alternative to traditional semantic feature analysis drills.

AAC

Conversation Word Bank

Input context about an upcoming conversation and the AI generates a tailored vocabulary bank on the spot. Useful for AAC users preparing for specific interactions — appointments, family events, unfamiliar situations — where having the right words ready in advance makes all the difference.
